1. If `p` varies the square of the product of u and v, and `p = -60` when `u = 3` and `v = 5`, find `u` when `p = -15` and `v = 21`
### Finding k
$$p = k(uv)^{2}$$
$$-60 = k(3 \cdot 5)^{2}$$
$$k=\frac{-60}{225}=\frac{-4}{15}$$
### Finding u
$$p = k(uv)^{2}$$
$$-15=(\frac{-4}{15})(u \cdot 21)^2$$
$$-225=-1764u^2$$
$$u^2=\frac{225}{1764}$$
$$u^2=\frac{25}{196}$$
$$\sqrt{u^2}=\sqrt{\frac{25}{196}}$$
$$u=\pm\frac{5}{14}$$
